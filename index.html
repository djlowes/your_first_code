Unlock the benefits of intermittent fasting

As the name suggests, intermittent fasting involves regulating your eating routine so you only consume meals between set intervals of the day or week. Two common fasting routines are:

  • 1:00pm - 5:00pm daily
  • Thursday - Monday weekly

Note: You must not consume food outside of these times. In the case of the weekly example above; no eating on Tuesday and Wednesday, at all.

Sounds Dumb.

It’s not. There is an extraordinary amount of scientific evidence showing the health benefits of fasting, ranging from general well-being, increased energy and weight loss through to cancer prevention. I have outlined a full list of benefits here. Religion has recognised the benefit of fasting for years - Judaism, Christianity and Islam all propose fasting during certain periods of the year. But let's let science tell the story:

Scientific Breakdown

Most people believe that fasting simply increases our caloric deficit, which causes us to lose weight. It's much more complex than that. To understand the benefits of fasting, we must understand how our metabolism works and the fascinating things that happen to our body when we deprive it of food.

*Click on the images below to enlage

1. Energy From Glucose: Our body uses food for energy. More specifically, our body’s cells use glucose as their default form of energy. We get glucose from carbohydrates (sugars and starchy foods).


2. Glucose Surplus: When we have enough glucose in our blood for energy, the surplus is stored in our liver as glycogen.


3. Glucose Deficit: When we don’t have enough glucose available to meet energy demands, our body adopts an alternative energy strategy and breaks down glycogen and fat stored in adipose tissue, which mobilizes free fatty acids into the blood to provide glucose from triglycerides.


4. Ketones: This process produces a wonderful thing called a ketone body. Ketones are used by our body as a new source of fuel, yielding enormous benefits.


5. Ketosis: In our fasted state metabolism we enter into ketosis, a biologicl sensation. Our heart, brain and many other organs function in a superior state. Fasting helps with our overall health!



The bottom line is that when you fast, your body initiates a cellular repair process. In addition to using ketones as a new energy source, your insulin levels drop, HGH (Human Growth Hormone) increases and gene expression changes. All of these things are associated with anti-aging and general well-being. If you are not fasting; start now.
